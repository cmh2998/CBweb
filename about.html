The mission

Community first, always

Cadence wants to put a message out into the latex world and wider: that we all belong. Fetish is not something we should hide, it's something we should celebrate and embrace.

Her hope is that her content, and that of the wonderful latex community around her, encourages others to be who they want to be, latex enthusiasts, aspirational fetishists, and all. Nothing here is about selling latex; it's about opening the door to it.

Then KG happened

“I hear you” and “I see you”

Cadence's world collided with KG's, and he introduced her to latex. He'd known from very young that he was “different”, and then felt he had to hide that part of himself his whole life.

She remembers the joy he felt the day she secretly bought latex and wore it for him. A way to say “I hear you” and “I see you”. That moment is the heart of everything built here: nobody should have to hide.

What Cadence builds

Two shops, one purpose

DeCadence is Cadence's contribution to making latex more accessible. She hand makes one-off items of latex clothing and gives them away, because a first piece of latex shouldn't depend on the price tag attached to it. Offcuts become chokers, cuffs, and other small accessories, so nothing goes to waste and there's a way in for everyone.

ReCadence is her way of supporting the community with access to latex while supporting worthwhile charities. Pre-loved garments are donated, cleaned and checked, then rehomed with someone who'll love them. 100% of every sale goes to charity, no exceptions.
